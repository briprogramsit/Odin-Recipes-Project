Lasagna



Description

The origins of the word lasagne or lasagna can be traced back to Ancient Greece. It is in reference to laganon, one of the earliest forms of pasta. The classic Italian dish that we have come to love however originated in Italy in the city of Naples in the 14th century.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Mozarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Ground beef
  • Onion
  • Garlic cloves
  • Tomato sauce
  • Italian seasoning
  • Ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg beaten

Instructions

  1. Heat oven to 350 degrees. Cook pasta al dente according to the package directions.
  2. Brown the beef, onion, garlic over medium heat. Drain any remaining grease.
  3. Stir in pasta sauce and Italian seasoning.
  4. Make cheese mixture by combining mozarella, parmesan, ricotta cheese and egg.
  5. Add cup of meat sauce to pan and layer with noodles and cheese mixture.
  6. Cover with foil and bake 45 minutes.
  7. Uncover, sprinkle with remaining cheese (mozarella and parmesan)
  8. Bake an addition 15 minutes before letting it cool.
  9. ENJOY!
